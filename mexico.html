Survival

Okay so the title of this post seems pretty radical, but I'm not joking when I say I've already encountered a few scenarios that could have so easily ended in disaster for me...

I write this post from the comfort of a cute cafe literally round the corner from my hotel, I'm talking less than a one minute walk away. I'm perfectly safe being sat here all by myself, but I can't say that's been true for the previous day in Mexico city.

Before I left I told myself some basic rules to not get kidnapped or myself killed while in South America: 1) don't talk to strangers who aren't on my tour. 2) don't get into taxis that aren't UBER or that I haven't booked. 3) don't drink the tap water. I know what you're thinking, how has this made it to the top 3 of Steph's survival rules? Well, funny story, I didn't get the vaccine which protects you if you pick up something from dodgy water. It was £50 and I wasn't prepared to part with that kinda money for an angry nurse to stab me which in turn will probably make me faint. No thank you.

But this is where the problem arises, within less than 1 hour of being in Mexico I did all of the above. Yes, ALL OF THEM. It took me an hour to get through immigration and when I got out the other side my lovely and safe transfer which I had booked was nowhere to be seen. I'd been awake nearly 24 hours and just wanted to be in my hotel room. So what did I do? I approached the first Mexican man I could find of course. He then sorted a taxi out for me and done, before I knew it I was in a taxi supposedly heading to my hotel, but I couldn't guarantee this because I don't actu,ally know a word of Spanish: I let this man I met all of 5 minutes ago put me in a taxi.

But here's the thing, and where only this situation would happen to me. He turned out to be a genuinely amazing guy. He wasn't dodgy. He didn't want to kidnap me. And he even showed me round Mexico city the following day. I don't know how I did it but I managed to find a decent Mexican man within an hour of arriving in Mexico. Who'd have known? So what have I already learnt about myself in these first 2 days? For one, I'm incredibly naïve and trusting for my own good. This guy could have turned out to be anyone and within my first day in South America I could have been a goner. Anthony, if you're reading this, thank you so much for not killing me, I will forever be eternally grateful for meeting such a lovely person. And secondly, just buy the goddamn bottled water and DEAL WITH IT.

I understand that this blog post isn't exactly reassuring to those who want to see me home safe and sound on 18th November. I still have another 73 days to survive in this crazy continent and if these past 2 days are anything to go by... I can't guarantee that. Sorry Tilly love you xxx

However, I managed to land on my feet and simultaneously reinforced the notion that spending tonnes of money on booked tours was 100% the right thing for me to do.

On that safe and reassuring note, I'm done. Here is my first few hours in Mexico perfectly summarised. In all honesty, I wouldn't have done anything differently and I'm not sorry for that! Love always, Steph x

PS, Anthony also got all the angles and endorsed my tourist vibes- LAD.
